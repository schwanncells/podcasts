# Code Switch — "The Scouts are too woke, according to Pete Hegseth"

**Hosts:** Gene Demby and B.A. Parker. **Guest:** Benjamin Rene Jordan (historian of American gender, environmental, and cultural history; author of *Modern Manhood and the Boy Scouts of America: Citizenship, Race and the Environment, 1910 to 1930*; currently researching scouting's role in early civil rights and environmental activism).

## TL;DR

- **Pete Hegseth pressured Scouting America to eliminate DEI policies and a 2020 "Citizenship in Society" merit badge** — gave the organization six months to comply or the Defense Department would withdraw support, including military base access and national forest camping privileges.
- **Boy Scouts membership collapsed from 10 million in 1970 to ~1 million today** — Hegseth blamed DEI, but historian Jordan argues the decline is structural: urbanization, youth counterculture shift, and organizational pivots in the 1970s and 1980s caused the drop, not recent inclusivity efforts.
- **The early Boy Scouts (1910s–1930s) were aggressively inclusive on race, religion, and ethnicity** — they explicitly defied the Ku Klux Klan's threats to withdraw funding unless they excluded Catholics, Jews, and African Americans; Boy Scouts publicly sided against the KKK on membership.
- **Boy Scouts rejected militarism despite its military-derived aesthetic** — unlike rival "American Boy Scouts" that armed kids with guns (resulting in a fatal shooting incident in the 1910s), the BSA positioned itself as civilian, emphasizing community service, civil rights activism, and environmental conservation.
- **Scouting America refused to exclude girls, trans, and gay members under Hegseth's pressure** — the national office issued a definitive statement maintaining inclusivity; local troop sponsors who disagreed already left (including the LDS Church), so remaining members are predominantly aligned with or indifferent to DEI policies.
- **Hegseth's forced military service badge has an ironic consequence** — trans, gay, and female scouts now required to complete a military service badge, potentially inspiring them to pursue actual military careers; Hegseth inadvertently positioned DEI-affirming scouting as a pipeline to military service.

**Scouting's Rise in the Modernizing America of the 1910s–1920s**

The Boy Scouts emerged in early 20th-century America as a response to rapid modernization. Urbanization, electrification, automobiles, and movies created anxiety about adolescence — a concept that didn't exist before 1900. Scouting filled a void by offering youth practical skills, outdoor experience, and civic engagement outside home, school, and church. The Scout Handbook became the highest-selling English-language book after the Bible. The organization grew to over 5 million members and was endorsed across the political spectrum, from Rockefellers and Roosevelts to socialists like Eugene Debs. Its appeal lay not in militarism but in combining outdoor adventure with active community service and early forms of civil rights activism.

**Boy Scouts as an Outlier on Race and Religion**

During an era of Jim Crow, lynching, and KKK ascendancy, the Boy Scouts stood apart. While some local troop leaders were discriminatory, the national organization deliberately and publicly pushed for inclusion of African Americans, Jewish people, Catholics, and immigrants. The organization even entered public debates with the Ku Klux Klan over membership. When the KKK threatened to withhold funding and membership unless the Scouts excluded Catholics, Jews, and Black Americans, national leadership told the Klan to leave. Jordan emphasizes this was framed as patriotism: true patriotism meant inclusive citizenship, not the Klan's vision of exclusion. The Girl Scouts, by contrast, lagged behind on racial inclusion until the 1940s–1950s, remaining predominantly white and WASP.

**Membership Decline and Structural Shifts, Not Cultural Decline**

Membership peaked and began declining by the 1960s, when urbanization was complete and youth culture shifted toward counterculture and anti-establishment skepticism. Scouting became associated with the establishment and traditional authority, making it a target for rebellion. The organization doubled down on urban outreach and DEI-oriented policies in the 1970s — a pivot that didn't reverse the decline. In the 1980s, during the Reagan era, the Scouts switched back to "traditional" positioning, but what emerged was narrower and less inclusive than the pre-1970s organization. This conservative rebranding, paired with legal battles (like the 2000 Supreme Court case defending the right to exclude gay leaders), created the perception that Boy Scouts were inherently exclusionary — a modern invention, not historical truth.

**The False Promise of Hegseth's "Traditional" Scouting**

Hegseth frames current DEI policies as a departure from "traditional good old-fashioned scouting," but Jordan argues this is historically inaccurate. Early scouting was not militaristic; it explicitly distanced itself from military youth organizations like the American Boy Scouts, which armed children and resulted in fatal accidents. The BSA received a federal charter in 1916 (the second in U.S. history, after the Red Cross) *because* it positioned itself as civilian, patriotic, and nonpartisan. Hegseth's militarism contradicts foundational scouting values. His demands — eliminating the citizenship badge, adding a military service badge, waiving fees for military families — overreach the actual scope of Defense Department involvement; 90% of Scouting America's benefits come from state and federal public lands, not the military.

**The Strategic Target and Its Ironic Outcome**

Jordan notes that Hegseth specifically targeted Scouting America rather than promoting obscure conservative scouting splinter groups because large, controversial organizations serve as platforms for culture-war messaging. Alternative organizations exist but remain marginal (around 30,000 members vs. 1 million+ for Scouting America). By putting national pressure on the Scouts, Hegseth created a high-profile battleground. However, the organization refused to budge on core inclusivity commitments. The paradoxical result: Scouting America is now a frontline DEI organization, functioning as a civic pipeline that welcomes girls, trans youth, and gay members into military service consideration. Far from dismantling DEI, Hegseth's military service badge requirement may inspire inclusive scouts to pursue actual military careers.

**What This Reveals About Partisan Governance**

The episode underscores a shift in federal governance norms. Historically, Cabinet officials like a Secretary of Defense maintained nonpartisanship, viewing their role as serving all citizens. Trump-appointed officials, including Hegseth, treat nonpartisanship as a liability and embrace explicit partisan positioning. This extends beyond scope: Hegseth's focus on scouting gender and DEI policies has little to do with the Defense Department's actual purview, suggesting the real objective is culture-war leverage over a visible national institution, not military readiness or stewardship of shared public lands.
